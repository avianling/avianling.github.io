Under Stranger Stars

Under Stranger Stars is an independent title I released in August 2019 for Steam.

During this project I acted as lead developer, taking responsibility for getting the job done and solving technical challenges.

In addition to traditional development responsibilities, I also presented Under Stranger Stars at the Armageddon Expo, the Chromacon Expo, showed the game at the 2018 GDC in San Francisco and ran a Kickstarter Campaign in 2019.

Technical Elements:

  • Creating physics solvers to support ships moving with damaged or broken parts
  • Camera systems
  • UI systems involving a mix of 2D and 3D objects
  • Shader and effect systems
  • Serialization
  • Multiplatform support (Pc + Xbox)
  • Controller Rebinding with hotswappable controllers
  • Integration of external systems - INK scripting language.
   

Legacy

Legacy is an experimental title focusing on AI and dynamic narrative. Featuring an advanced simulation engine, Legacy is able to create a living world where characters react to the world around them, adapting their goals, actions & personalities to their unique situations.

On this title my responsibilities included creating AI, Unity editor extensions and game architectures which could support the complex requirements of the project.

Technical Elements:

  • Utility & Awareness based AI systems.
  • Scalable AI systems using scheduling & LoD.
  • Dynamic 2D shadows via mesh generation.
  • Day/Night cycles.
  • Runtime navmesh generation.
  • Unity based content & AI editors.

Endless Adventure

Endless Adventure was an open world, procedurally generated mobile title I worked on at Ninja Kiwi. The goal was to create a minimal, exploration focused game with a unique visual style.

We also used a textureless approach using SVGs and mesh generation to provide extremely fast load times and performance.

Technical Elements:

  • Gameplay Programming.
  • Procedural generation of characters, environments.
  • Procedural Animation.
  • Open World game architecture.
  • Visual Effects.
  • UI Programming.
  • Ninja Kiwi backend integration.

Fortress: Destroyer

Fortress: Destroyer is a mobile title I developed at Ninja Kiwi featuring 3D ship combat and collection mechanics. This was Ninja Kiwis first 3D game and first Unity project.

For this project I worked in a medium sized team developing gameplay features, game architectures, and supporting other team members by building editor extensions.

Technical Elements:

  • Gameplay Programming.
  • Destructible terrain.
  • UI Systems.
  • Special Effects & Shaders.
  • AI
  • Live Ops integration.
  • Unity Editors - terrain builder & terrain painter.
