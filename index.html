I AM A PROGRAMMER

FEATURED PROJECT

I have been programming since I was 12. I studied software engineering at the University of Auckland, graduating with honours in 2013, and I work at NinjaKiwi developing unity games. I like to experiment with new ideas by creating prototypes. Recently I have expanded my knowledge of procedural generation, open world and streaming technologies and circular coordinate systems.

While building Fortress: Destroyer, I rapidly created the initial prototype, then played a major role in the development team. My work on Fortress included a well performing, highly destructable terrain system, UI framework and highly flexible combat backend.

I AM A DESIGNER

FEATURED PROJECT

I design and create games. I push my boundaries by creating different experiences with different techniques. I study existing games, and participate in community discussions on game design. I am particularly interested in creating emergent narrative and plot, and in understanding how game elements drive human emotion. I improve these skills via experimentation, such as designing abilities and enemies in Fortress: Destroyer, designing puzzles in Rocket Maze and creating the universe in One Mans Sky.

Inspired by No Man's Sky, I created a procedural universe called One Mans Sky. Creating a universe where you simultaneously feel the size of the galaxy and that it is all within reach was a unique design challenge which I solved by creating landmarks, a sense of scale and the feeling of immense acceleration.

I AM A LEARNER

FEATURED PROJECT

I continue to grow my skill base through research, experimentation, conferences and online discussion. I review games on the reddit r/gamedesign forum, I attend game jams regularly, and judge the university of auckland SESA game jam.
